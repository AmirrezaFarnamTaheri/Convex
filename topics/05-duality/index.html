05. Duality: Lagrangian, KKT, Strong Duality

Date: 2025-11-18 · Duration: 90 min · Tags: duality, theory

Overview: Dual problems, optimality conditions, shadow prices, and why duality powers algorithms.

Prerequisites: Lecture 04

Learning Objectives

After this lecture, you should understand:

  • How to form the Lagrangian and the dual problem for a convex optimization problem.
  • The Karush-Kuhn-Tucker (KKT) conditions for optimality.
  • The concept of strong duality and its implications.

Key Concepts

Concept 1: The Lagrangian

The Lagrangian of a standard form convex optimization problem is $L(x, \lambda, \nu) = f_0(x) + \sum_{i=1}^m \lambda_i f_i(x) + \sum_{j=1}^p \nu_j h_j(x)$.

Visual intuition: The Lagrangian incorporates the constraints into the objective function.
