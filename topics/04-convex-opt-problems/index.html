04. Convex Optimization Problems: Standard Forms

Date: 2025-11-11 · Duration: 90 min · Tags: standard-forms, classification

Overview: LP, QP, SDP, SOCP—how to recognize and formulate convex problems.

Learning Objectives

After this lecture, you should understand:

  • The standard form of a convex optimization problem.
  • How to identify and formulate Linear Programs (LPs), Quadratic Programs (QPs), and Semidefinite Programs (SDPs).
  • The importance of problem classification in optimization.

Key Concepts

Concept 1: The Standard Form

A convex optimization problem is in standard form if it is written as: Minimize $f_0(x)$ Subject to $f_i(x) \le 0, i=1, \dots, m$ $h_j(x) = 0, j=1, \dots, p$ where $f_0, \dots, f_m$ are convex functions and $h_1, \dots, h_p$ are affine.

Visual intuition: We are minimizing a convex function over a convex set.
